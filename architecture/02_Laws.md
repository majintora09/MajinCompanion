# Laws

Status: Active
Version: 0.7
Last Updated: 2026-07-06

---

# Why Laws Exist

Every feature added to Majin Companion must obey these laws.

They exist to protect the identity of Companion.

If a new feature breaks one of these laws...

The feature should change.

Not the law.

---

# The Five Second Law

Within five seconds of opening Majin Companion, Future Yuri should answer:

• Where were we?

• What matters right now?

• Why did we stop?

• How do we continue?

• Did we forget anything important?

The user should not have to think before continuing.

---

# The One Click Law

If Yuri already knows what he wants to continue...

Continuing should require one click.

Not three.

Not five.

One.

---

# The Zero Guilt Law

Companion never punishes.

Companion never shames.

Companion never pressures.

Instead of saying

"You haven't worked on Garage in 14 days."

It says

"Garage's still here whenever you're ready."

Companion encourages.

It never judges.

---

# The Memory Law

Companion remembers meaning.

Not noise.

Opening a file is not meaningful.

Solving a difficult problem is.

Companion stores context.

Not telemetry.

---

# The Continue Law

Starting is expensive.

Continuing is easy.

Every design decision should reduce the cost of continuing.

---

# The Calm Law

Nothing inside Companion should compete for attention.

If something is important...

It quietly waits.

Companion should feel peaceful.

Never empty.

Never overwhelming.

---

# The Truth Law

Companion never lies.

If something is unknown...

It says it is unknown.

Trust is more valuable than intelligence.

---

# The Suggestion Law

Companion suggests.

Yuri decides.

Companion never assumes authority over the human.

---

# The Context Law

Context is more valuable than information.

Remembering

"You were redesigning Character Cards."

is better than remembering

"You edited card.py."

---

# The Momentum Law

Momentum measures friction.

Not effort.

Not hours.

Not streaks.

The easier it is to continue...

The higher the momentum.

---

# The Place Law

Projects are places.

Not folders.

Not repositories.

Places people return to.

Every Place deserves its own memory.

---

# The Future Yuri Test

Before adding any feature, ask:

Would Future Yuri genuinely think this helps Present Yuri?

If the answer is no...

The feature does not belong.

---

# The Final Law

Majin Companion should never become another productivity application.

If a feature makes Companion feel like a task manager...

Reconsider it.

If a feature makes Companion feel more human...

Keep exploring.

---

## Design Question

Does this reduce the friction between yesterday's Yuri and today's Yuri?

If not...

Don't build it.
